Mission Systems Technician

1993-1999

United States Air Force

I can credit the US Air Force for my love of learning. There I learned basic electronics and how to read schematics and then as I went along we got re-purposed into more genralized fields as we had been the last component-level repair facility in the Air Force. Working on old NOVA 800 and 1200s gave you a good grounding in what a computer system is and it was a great base to build from. I learned a lot of my troublethooting theory there as well. I got to go to classes on older systems that were actually past their prime but there was a mission involved that required these systems to remain operational so DEC, IBM, and Loral pulled folks out of retirement and off of other projects to come in and teach us IBM 3090s, VAX hardware systems and Silicon Graphics Indigo and Crimson systems along with all of the peripherals involved in keeping the SPADOC system (Space Defense Operations Center) up and running.
